Knots¶

Knot Science! No, but it is science! Low tech science. The science of knots! The practice of getting a grip on things. Blood knots, nail knots, uni knots, loop knots, clinch knots. With knot making techniques, it is possible to utilize the amazing potential of cordage and manipulate nature!

What is amazing is the low tech nature of cordage. Cordage can be created from plant fiber, animal hair fiber, and silk critter fiber. A trip to the woods or your nearest animal, in some situations, will suffice to form cordage. With time and patience, somewhat fragile fibers can be weaved into something with strength.

Knots have a purpose: object to object attachment or a mechanism that tightens when pulled. Knots are what we can use to get a grip on things. Grip a hook, grip a rock or weight, grip another cordage. With some time and construction, create a net configuration to trap and grip a fish. Create a snare or slip knot configuration to trap and grip a land animal.

Knots are a way to bond objects without glue, without the need of welding, and without the construction of modern nails and screws. Bond any solid object to any other solid object. Tether wood to metal, wood to wood, stone to wood, animal to tree, tree to tree, fish to human, etc. What a leap we made as people with the invention of the knot. An invention so phenomenal, that it’s usage persists, even amidst modern technology in current times.
